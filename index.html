How Much Does Your State Fund Public Education?

Each state in the U.S. differs in the amount per student it spends on public school education.

According to data from the National Center for Education Statistics, there is a large difference in the amount per student each state funds public education. In some cases, a student in one state could be receiving 1/3 the amount of funding as a student in another state. Explore the map below.

Depending on the district, a student in Utah could be receiving $15,000 less than a student in New York.

Four states spend less than $8,000 per student, with Utah spending the least at $7,006.

Five states spend over than $19,000 per student, with New York spending the most at $21,818.

Explore this map to find out how much your state spends by hovering over it.

SOURCE: National Center for Education Statistics

According to an article by the National Center for Science and Engineering Statistics, current expenditures by public schools include instruction and related costs, student support services, administration, and operations and exclude funds for school construction and other capital outlays, debt service, and programs outside of public elementary and secondary education.

According to an article by Governing, there are many reasons why states differ in public school education funding.
